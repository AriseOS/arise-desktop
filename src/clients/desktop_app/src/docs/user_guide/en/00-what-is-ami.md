 # What is Ami?
 
 ## One-sentence overview
 
 **Ami is an AI assistant that learns how you work by watching you complete a task once, then repeats it for you.**
 
 You do not need to write scripts or draw flowcharts. You just work in the browser as usual; Ami learns, remembers, and repeats the way you do it.
 
 ---
 
 ## What problems does it solve for you?
 
 If you often find yourself doing things like:
 
 - Visiting the same websites over and over to copy data into spreadsheets;
 - Clicking through internal dashboards every day or week to download reports;
 - Copying and pasting information between multiple pages to reconcile or clean up lists;
 - Repeating almost the same steps every time you launch a new campaign or run a similar analysis;
 
 then Ami is designed to let you **do a clean “demo run” once, and let it handle the repetition afterward**.
 
 ---
 
 ## How does Ami work? (from your point of view)
 
 You can think of Ami as a digital coworker that can **watch, learn, and do**:
 
 ### 1. You do one demonstration (recording)
 
 1. In Ami Desktop, click **Start Recording**.
 2. In the browser, complete one full run of your task as you normally would: search, click, type, copy the data you care about.
 3. When you are done, click **Stop Recording**.
 
 During this process, Ami records what you do, for example:
 
 - Which pages you open (navigation);
 - Which buttons and links you click;
 - What you type or paste into input fields and forms;
 - Which pieces of text you highlight (often the data you care about);
 - Some scrolling and viewport changes (to help replay the same context later).
 
 ### 2. Ami turns that run into a Workflow
 
 - After you stop recording, Ami automatically analyzes what task you were trying to complete;
 - It breaks your actions into individual steps (open page, filter, collect data, export, and so on);
 - It generates a reusable **workflow** that can be executed multiple times.
 
 In the UI you can see the rough structure: how many steps there are, what each step roughly does, and what goes in and out.
 
 ### 3. You review once, then let it run on its own
 
 - Skim the workflow to see if anything looks wrong or if any fields are missing;
 - If you are happy with it, save the workflow;
 - Next time you want to do the same job, just click **Run**. Ami will open the browser and walk through the same process you taught it, then present the results to you.
 
 ---
 
 ## Beyond “watch once, learn once”: other ways Ami can help
 
 Inside the desktop app, there are a few typical usage patterns:
 
 ### 1. Teach Ami by recording (quick start)
 
 Best when you already know exactly how to do something, but you are tired of repeating it:
 
 - For example: scraping product information from an e-commerce site;
 - Exporting a standard report from an internal dashboard;
 - Cleaning or restructuring a list in a predictable way.
 
 ### 2. Let AI “design” a workflow for you via conversation
 
 Sometimes you just have a goal in mind, but not a fixed step-by-step process, for example:
 
 - “I want to automatically download the sales report every morning and email it to myself.”
 - “I want to regularly collect prices and stock levels for several competitors and keep a comparison table.”
 
 In that case, you can go to **AI Workflow Generation** and describe your goal in natural language. Ami will:
 
 - Ask clarifying questions (which site, which fields, how to save the data, and so on);
 - Propose a workflow plan that could achieve this goal;
 - Generate a runnable workflow that you can then inspect and run.
 
 ### 3. Fix workflows by talking to AI
 
 If a workflow does not behave as expected, or fails on certain pages, you can:
 
 - Ask Ami to explain it first: “Tell me what this workflow is doing step by step.”
 - Describe what is wrong: “Step 2 only saves price, but I also need stock,” or “This step fails on some products with a missing element error.”
 - Let AI propose changes and update the workflow;
 - Run it again, see if it matches your expectations, and iterate through this conversational loop as needed.

 ## Who is Ami for? Typical use cases
 
 Ami is built for **knowledge workers who often perform repetitive tasks in the browser**, for example:
 
 - **E-commerce operations / merchandisers**:
   - Regularly scraping prices, stock, reviews, and sales from marketplaces;
   - Building competitor comparison sheets and monitoring price changes.
 - **Data analysts / BI**:
   - Exporting data from multiple internal dashboards every day or week;
   - Normalizing and loading data into Excel or a database.
 - **Growth / marketing**:
   - Collecting campaign information, rankings, media or social data;
   - Maintaining lead lists, follow-up lists, or simple records.
 - **General office work**:
   - Repeatedly filling forms, checking information, and cross-referencing data across internal systems.
 
 **In short**: whenever you find yourself “doing nearly the same clicks in the browser again and again,” Ami can become your digital junior assistant for that part of your job.
 
 ---
 
 ## How is Ami different from traditional automation or generic agents?
 
 - **You do not write scripts; it “watches you do it once.”**  
   Traditional RPA or scripting tools ask you to design flows. Generic AI agents ask you to design long prompts. Ami asks you to simply perform the task once, then it learns and structures that behavior for you.
 
 - **You do not get a one-off script; you get a workflow that can be refined and evolved.**  
   When you notice issues, you can tell AI in plain language what is wrong and what you want instead. The workflow can be updated and improved over time, rather than rebuilt from scratch.
 
 - **It is not just a one-off chat; it remembers how you work.**  
   A workflow you create once can be run, reused, and combined with others. Over time, you will build a small “team” of workflows that take over the repetitive parts of your work.
 
 ---
 
 ## What do you need to get started?
 
 The setup on your side is very light:
 
 1. A computer where you can install Ami Desktop (see the download page for supported platforms).
 2. Access to the websites and systems you normally use for your tasks (including any required logins).
 3. One or two tasks you do repeatedly every day or week that you would most like to hand off.
 
 Ami will take care of the rest: recording your actions, turning them into a workflow, and replaying them for you.
